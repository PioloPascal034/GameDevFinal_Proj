The world fades in slowly white lights, antiseptic air, the soft hum of machines.
He wakes up lying on a hospital bed. His vision swims. His body refuses to move.
He tries to speak, but only a shallow breath escapes.

A nurse standing beside him gasps softly.

“He’s awake…” she whispers, almost afraid, before rushing to the door.
Moments later, she returns with a doctor whose footsteps echo coldly across the floor.

The doctor stops at the foot of the bed, observing him with unsettling calm.
No warmth. No relief. Just… calculation.

He leans forward.

“Outstanding performance.”
A pause. A faint smile.
“Subject_09.”

The words hit harder than any injury.
His chest tightens—not from pain, but from fear.
He doesn’t know his name.
He doesn’t know where he is.
He doesn’t even know if he ever escaped…

But the doctor’s eyes tell him one thing:

This was never a rescue.
This was part of the experiment.
